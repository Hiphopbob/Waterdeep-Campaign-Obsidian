Felix Waystone was raised on the roads that wind between the cities and towns of the Sword Coast. His earliest memories are not of walls or towers, but of wagon wheels rolling over packed earth, canvas snapping in the wind, and long stretches of road disappearing over distant hills. The caravans he grew up among carried timber, cloth, spices, tools, and whatever else merchants believed could turn a profit in the next settlement. Life on the road required everyone to contribute, and Felix learned early that survival depended on vigilance and cooperation.

As a young cub he handled simple tasks around the caravan: tending animals, carrying supplies, and running messages between wagon drivers. While others focused on trade and negotiation, Felix found himself drawn to the edges of the caravan line, watching the tree line and listening to the rhythms of the road. He developed a habit of noticing small details like broken branches, unusual tracks, and the sudden quiet of birds that often warned of trouble before it arrived.

As he grew older, the caravan masters began trusting him with greater responsibility. In his later teenage years he was allowed to scout ahead of the wagons, moving quietly along the road to watch for hazards, bandits, or dangerous wildlife. At first he carried only simple tools and a shortbow used more for signaling than fighting, but the experience taught him patience and awareness. Over time, his steady instincts and sharp eyesight earned him a place among those responsible for keeping danger at a distance from the caravan.

Eventually Felix was trusted with a proper weapon and assigned to assist the guards who protected the caravan during its journeys. His role was not to lead battles but to prevent them—watching from higher ground, keeping an eye on the surrounding terrain, and warning the others if something moved where it should not. The road offered many lessons: sometimes danger could be avoided entirely, sometimes it needed to be driven away quickly and cleanly.

Within the past year Felix began accepting small contracts outside the caravans he grew up with. Merchants occasionally needed an extra escort for a short stretch of road, a warehouse needed someone to keep watch overnight, or a traveler wanted a capable scout to guide them between towns. These were not grand jobs, but they allowed Felix to begin building a reputation of his own. He approached the work carefully, choosing contracts that focused on protection and observation rather than needless violence. The work suited him well enough, though he knew he was still finding his place in the trade.

Several of these small contracts brought him in and out of Waterdeep. The city was louder and more crowded than any place he had known growing up, but repeated visits gradually made it less unfamiliar. He became comfortable navigating the caravan yards, outer gates, and dockside warehouses where merchants gathered to arrange their next journeys.

His most recent job was meant to be another step forward. After escorting a caravan north, he had arranged to return to Waterdeep and immediately begin another contract. The first journey went smoothly, but the second job collapsed before it ever began. The client withdrew from the agreement without explanation, leaving Felix in the city without the work he had expected.

Felix is not without coin, but neither is he well established. For now he remains in Waterdeep, hoping to find new opportunities and continue building a name for himself. He follows a simple personal code shaped by years on the road: protect those who place their trust in him, avoid cruelty toward animals and the defenseless, and treat allies as part of the same hunting pack rather than competitors.

Felix Waystone is still early in his path. He has skill, patience, and the instincts of a capable scout, but he knows there is much more to learn. For the moment, Waterdeep is simply another stop along the road—a place where a young ranger might find the next opportunity to prove himself.
